Introduction to Stableswap

Stableswap is a specialized exchange mechanism designed to provide highly efficient swaps between $ORDER and other stablecoins. Unlike traditional AMMs that use a constant product formula (x*y=k), Stableswap employs a specialized invariant curve optimized for assets that should trade at or near parity.

This feature will enable users to easily move between $ORDER and other stablecoins (such as USDC, USDT, and DAI) with minimal slippage, even for large trades, while providing additional utility and liquidity for the $ORDER token.

Core Concepts

Specialized Curve for Stablecoins

Stableswap utilizes a modified StableSwap invariant curve, similar to those pioneered by Curve Finance, but with specific optimizations for $ORDER:

Comparison of Stableswap curve (blue) vs Constant Product curve (red)

The key features of the curve include:

  • Concentrated Liquidity: Most liquidity is concentrated around the 1:1 price ratio, providing excellent swap efficiency
  • Dynamic Amplification Factor: Adjusts based on market conditions to optimize stability
  • Asymmetric Design: Special parameters to account for $ORDER's unique backing mechanism
  • Low Fee Structure: Just 0.04% swap fee, significantly lower than traditional AMMs

Peg Maintenance Mechanisms

To help maintain $ORDER's peg to $1, the Stableswap includes several specialized mechanisms:

  • Dynamic Fees: Fee rates adjust based on deviation from the peg
  • Arbitrage Incentives: Protocol incentives for arbitrageurs who help restore the peg
  • Protocol-Owned Liquidity: A portion of protocol treasury will provide baseline liquidity
  • Redemption Backstop: $ORDER can always be redeemed against collateral at floor price
