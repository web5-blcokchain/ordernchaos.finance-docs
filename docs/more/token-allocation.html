Token Allocation

How tokens are allocated in the OrderNChaos ecosystem

Previous: Addresses Next: Fees

Last updated 3 years ago

Token Allocation Philosophy

Zero Pre-Allocation Model

OrderNChaos follows a radically fair distribution model with zero pre-allocation to team, investors, or insiders. Every token is backed by real value and can only be created through market mechanisms or protocol participation.

$CHAOS & $ORDER are each token with intrinsic value. They cannot be minted out of thin air, and they never are. All $CHAOS tokens require at least their floor value's worth of liquidity in the protocol-owned market. All $ORDER tokens require at least their notional value of the collateral ($CHAOS) locked in the protocol.

There are no "team allocations" of $ORDER, $CHAOS, or $prCHAOS tokens nor an "emission schedule" for their creation. OrderNChaos does not have a "fully diluted value."

The protocol development, marketing, content-creation, and bug bounty campaigns are all funded through sustainable protocol fees, creating a self-sustaining ecosystem without requiring token inflation.

Initial Distribution: Genesis Launch Auction (GLA)

The Genesis Launch Auction (GLA) is the only source of initial $CHAOS tokens. This fair launch mechanism has the following characteristics:

  • Total Initial Supply: 1,000,000 $CHAOS tokens available through the GLA
  • Duration: 7 days (168 hours)
  • Participation: Open to anyone, with equal access for all participants
  • Minimum Price: Starting at $1 per $CHAOS
  • Maximum Price: Determined by market demand during the auction
  • Price Increase: Price increases linearly throughout the auction

All funds raised in the GLA are permanently locked in the protocol as the initial liquidity reserve, establishing the irreversible floor price for $CHAOS.

Token Supply Mechanics

$CHAOS Supply

The supply of $CHAOS can only increase when:

  • New liquidity is added to the protocol-owned market
  • The price of $CHAOS increases above its floor value
  • Users purchase $CHAOS tokens from the protocol
  • Users convert $prCHAOS rewards to $CHAOS (minus a 2% fee that increases the floor price)

Key supply characteristics:

  • Initial Supply: 1,000,000 $CHAOS (from GLA)
  • Maximum Supply: Unlimited, but growth is constrained by market demand and value added to the protocol
  • Supply Growth Rate: Directly correlated with protocol usage and market demand

$ORDER Supply

The supply of $ORDER can only increase when:

  • Users mint new $ORDER loans by using their staked $CHAOS as collateral
  • The loans are always collateralized at 1:1 ratio with the floor value of $CHAOS

Key supply characteristics:

  • Initial Supply: 0 $ORDER (minted only as users create loans)
  • Maximum Supply: Limited by the total amount of staked $CHAOS and its floor value
  • Supply Growth Rate: Directly correlated with user borrowing demand

$ORDER Collateralization

Unlike traditional stablecoins, $ORDER is always fully collateralized by $CHAOS at its floor price. This means that even if the market price of $CHAOS falls, the collateralization ratio remains intact, eliminating liquidation risk.

$prCHAOS Supply

The supply of $prCHAOS increases:

  • At regular intervals based on the total supply of $CHAOS
  • These rewards are distributed to $CHAOS stakers proportionally to their stake

Key supply characteristics:

  • Initial Supply: 0 $prCHAOS
  • Emission Rate: 50% APR (of staked $CHAOS) in $prCHAOS rewards
  • Conversion Rate: Variable, starting at 2.5 $prCHAOS = 1 $CHAOS
  • Conversion Fee: 2% (goes to increasing the floor price)

Funding the Protocol

Rather than allocating tokens for the team or investors, OrderNChaos uses a sustainable fee model to fund ongoing development and operations:

Fee Sources

  • Trading Fees: 0.5% on all trades through the protocol-owned market
    • 80% goes to increasing the floor price
    • 20% goes to the protocol treasury
  • Loan Fees: 0.1% one-time fee on minting $ORDER
    • 50% goes to increasing the floor price
    • 50% goes to the protocol treasury
  • Conversion Fees: 2% fee when converting $prCHAOS to $CHAOS
    • 100% goes to increasing the floor price
  • Stableswap Fees (Coming Soon): 0.04% on stablecoin swaps
    • 50% distributed to stakers
    • 50% goes to the protocol treasury

Treasury Allocation

The protocol treasury funds are allocated according to the following guidelines:

Treasury Fund Allocation

40% - Development
25% - Security
20% - Marketing
15% - Community
  • Development (40%): Funding for protocol improvements, new features, and technical infrastructure
  • Security (25%): Ongoing security audits, bug bounties, and formal verification of smart contracts
  • Marketing (20%): Education, content creation, and awareness campaigns
  • Community (15%): Grants, incentives, and community-led initiatives

This approach ensures that the protocol remains sustainable long-term without relying on token inflation or team allocations that could dilute holders.

Governance Transition

As outlined in the Roadmap, the protocol will gradually transition to decentralized community governance. When this transition occurs, treasury allocations will be determined by governance votes, allowing the community to direct resources as needed.
